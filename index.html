Each player gets a hidden role card, while three additional cards are placed face-down in the center of the table. After everyone has secretly looked at their own role, the night phase begins. The most important rule to remember is: One Night, One Day. Unlike other social deduction games, there is only one single night and one single day. Nobody is eliminated until the very end.

During the night, the Werewolves wake up to identify each other, and most Villager roles perform a special action. For example, the Troublemaker may swap the cards of two other players without them knowing.

When everyone has finished their night actions, all players wakes up. It is now day. Players discuss who they are, what they did, and who might be a Werewolf. This is the heart of the game: Villagers try to find a Werewolf, while the Werewolves lie about their identity to stay hidden.

When the day ends, everyone votes for another player. The player with the most votes dies. If at least one Werewolf dies, the Villagers win. If all Werewolves survive, the Werewolf team wins. Since a round usually takes only 5 to 15 minutes, there is always enough time for another game!

General Rules

  • Roles: Players only perform the action of the role they saw when the game begins. (Exceptions: Copycat, Doppelganger)
  • Reloading: If you reload the page during the night you might not get your info or do your night action.
